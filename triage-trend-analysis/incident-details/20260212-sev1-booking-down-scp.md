## Summary

Incident produced by a SCP Policy change applying to all Users and Services in PROD and PR201 AWS environments. This caused all AWS actions from services to fail due to privilege failures.

## Root Cause

The `FullAWSAccess` Service Control Policy was removed from the `ProdApplication` OU in the AWS Organization, with the understanding that the identical attachment to the parent OU and org root would inherit permissions.

**Key insight:** SCPs (and the related RCPs) operate differently from other management policies — they must be applied at every level, regardless of inheritance. The net effect was that **ALL access was briefly blocked**.

### What Happened
1. SRE proceeded with cleanup on duplicate SCP policies attached to different Org levels
2. Assumed duplicate policies at parent OU were not needed
3. Removal caused massive failures on all Users and Services connecting to AWS resources
4. Practically all AWS operations were affected
5. After policies were reattached to the Organization, services recovered

## Business Impact

- **208 PE Bookings / 435 total bookings lost**
- **Revenue Impact:** $13,610
- **Human Impact:** $935

## Detection

- Multiple on-call teams received different PagerDuty alerts and Sentry errors
- Detected within 1 minute
- First incident was triggered from a failure in Prod Push

## What Created Friction

1. Not having a Testing environment for SCP actions
2. Communication on SCP changes
3. Duplicate incident created, requiring cancellation of second one
4. Alert for SRE was grouped under lower severity alert
5. No mechanism for devs to force restart on their lambdas (required SRE action)
6. Detection of Database Migration failures was delayed
7. Jumpbox privilege checks created delays for DLQ redrive

## Key Lessons

1. **SCPs do not inherit like other AWS policies** — must be applied at every OU level
2. Need staging environment for testing organization-level policy changes
3. Should enforce Change Management for changes that can affect production
4. Include validation steps along with manual infrastructure changes
